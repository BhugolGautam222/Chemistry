Aldehyde and Ketone

Multiple Choice Questions

1. The general formula for aldehyde is:

  • a) CnH2n+2
  • b) CnH2No
  • c) CnH2NO2
  • d) CnH2n+2O (Correct Answer)

2. Which of the following gives ethanol on ozonolysis?

  • a) 2-butene (Correct Answer)
  • b) ethane
  • c) propene
  • d) 2,3-dimethyl but-2-ene

Reaction: CH3-CH=CH-CH3 + O3 → 2 CH3CH2OH (ethanol)

**Mechanism:**

1. Ozone adds to the double bond, forming a molozonide intermediate.

2. The molozonide rearranges into an ozonide.

3. Hydrolysis of the ozonide with water gives ethanol.

3. Which of the following compounds cannot reduce Fehling’s reagent?

  • a) HCHO
  • b) C6H5CHO (Correct Answer)
  • c) CH3CHO
  • d) CH3CH(CH3)CHO

Explanation: C6H5CHO (aromatic aldehyde) lacks an enediol intermediate.

**Mechanism:**

1. Fehling’s solution reacts with aldehydes via their enediol form.

2. Aromatic aldehydes, such as benzaldehyde (C6H5CHO), cannot form this intermediate and therefore cannot reduce Fehling’s solution.

Short Answer Questions

1. Write the functional isomer of propanal. How can you prepare the isomer from alcohol? What is Rosenmund’s reduction?

Functional isomer of propanal: 2-propanone (acetone).

**Mechanism:**

1. Propanal (CH3CH2CHO) and acetone (CH3COCH3) are functional isomers because they share the molecular formula C3H6O but differ in the functional group.

Rosenmund’s reduction: CH3COCl + H2 (Zn/HCl) → CH3CHO.

**Mechanism:**

1. Acid chloride reacts with hydrogen gas in the presence of zinc and hydrochloric acid.

2. The reaction reduces the acid chloride to an aldehyde.

Long Answer Questions

1. Reaction Sequences and Identification:

An alkene A on ozonolysis gives acetone and aldehyde. The aldehyde oxidizes to an acid B, which reacts with Br2 (red phosphorus) to yield C. Compound C reacts with NaOH to form hydroxy acid D. Acid D is also obtained from acetone via HCN reaction followed by acidic hydrolysis.

Detailed Reaction Sequence

**Mechanism:**

1. Ozonolysis of CH3CH=CH2 gives CH3CHO (aldehyde) and CH3COCH3 (acetone).

2. CH3CHO oxidizes to CH3COOH (acetic acid).

3. CH3COOH reacts with Br2 to form CH2BrCOOH (bromoacetic acid).

4. CH2BrCOOH reacts with NaOH to form HOCH2COOH (glycolic acid).
