2. The reagent used to change nitromethane to methylhydroxylamine is:

  • a) Zn/NaOH
  • b) Sn/HCl
  • c) Zn/NH4Cl
  • d) Zn/HCl (Correct Answer)

Reaction: CH3NO2 + Zn/HCl → CH3NHOH (Methylhydroxylamine)

**Mechanism:**

1. Nitro compound is reduced stepwise using Zn/HCl.

2. The intermediate nitroso compound is further reduced to hydroxylamine.

3. Reduction of nitrobenzene in neutral medium yields:

  • a) Nitrosobenzene
  • b) Phenylhydroxylamine (Correct Answer)
  • c) Azobenzene
  • d) Hydrazobenzene

Reaction: C6H5NO2 + Zn/NH4Cl → C6H5NHOH

**Mechanism:**

1. Nitrobenzene undergoes partial reduction to form nitrosobenzene.

2. Further reduction produces phenylhydroxylamine in a neutral medium.

Short Answer Questions

1. Why is nitrobenzene less reactive than benzene in electrophilic substitution reactions?

Answer: Nitrobenzene is less reactive because the nitro group (-NO2) is an electron-withdrawing group. It deactivates the benzene ring by reducing electron density and directs substitution to the meta position.

2. An organic compound A on refluxing with iodine in the presence of red phosphorus forms B. B reacts with ethanolic silver nitrite to form C, which reacts with sodium nitrite and HCl to get D. Alkaline D is blood red in color. Identify A, B, C, and D with reactions.

Answer:

  • A = CH3CH2NO2 (Ethyl nitro compound)
  • B = CH3CH2I (Ethyl iodide)
  • C = CH3CH=NOH (Pseudonitrol)
  • D = Blood-red colored salt of nitrolic acid

**Reactions:**

1. CH3CH2NO2 + I2 (red P) → CH3CH2I

2. CH3CH2I + AgNO2 → CH3CH=NOH

3. CH3CH=NOH + NaNO2/HCl → Blood-red D

Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the reduction of nitrobenzene in different media.

**Neutral Medium:**

Zn + NH4Cl → Phenylhydroxylamine (C6H5NHOH).

**Alkaline Medium:**

Zn + NaOH → Azoxybenzene (C6H5N=NO).

**Acidic Medium:**

Zn + HCl → Aniline (C6H5NH2).
